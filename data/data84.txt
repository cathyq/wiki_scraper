"{{use mdy dates|date=August 2015}}\n'''Margalit Fox''' (born 1961) is an American writer for ''[[The New York Times]]'', and other publications, and is a book author. She has written more than 1,200 obituaries for the ''Times''.\n\n==Biography==\nFox was born in [[Glen Cove, New York]], the daughter of David (a physicist) and Laura Fox.<ref>[http://www.encyclopedia.com/article-1G2-3072200090/fox-margalit-1961.html \"Fox, Margalit\"], Encyclopedia.com</ref>  She attended [[Barnard College]] in New York City and then [[Stony Brook University]], where she completed her bachelor's degree (1982) and then [[master's degree]] in linguistics in 1983.  She received a master's degree from the [[Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism]] in 1991.<ref name=Talkinghandsbook>[http://www.talkinghandsbook.com/theauthor.html \"About the Author\"], TalkingHandsBook.com, accessed June 16, 2013</ref>\n\nFox is a senior writer at ''[[The New York Times]]'', where she wrote book reviews and, since 2004, more than 1,200 obituaries.<ref>Davis, Simon. [http://www.vice.com/read/its-easy-to-die-but-not-easy-to-get-your-obituary-in-the-new-york-times-125 \"How the ''New York Times'' Churns Out 1,000 Obituaries Every Year\"], Vice.com, January 5, 2015</ref> She has written widely on language, culture and ideas for ''The Times'', ''[[New York Newsday]]'', ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' and other publications.  Her work was anthologized in ''Best Newspaper Writing, 2005''.<ref name=Talkinghandsbook/><ref name=Stanford>Karampelas, Gabrielle. [http://news.stanford.edu/news/2014/august/saroyan-award-winners-082114.html \"Margalit Fox and Kiese Laymon win Stanford's 2014 Saroyan Prize for Writing\"], ''Stanford News'',  Stanford University, August 21, 2014</ref>\n\nIn 2011, The Newswomen's Club of New York awarded Fox its Front Page Award for her collection of work at ''The New York Times''.<ref>{{cite web|title=2011 Winners| url=http://www.newswomensclubnewyork.com/2011-winners/| publisher=Newswomen's Club of New York|accessdate=7 October 2012}}</ref> In 2014, she won [[Stanford University]]'s [[William Saroyan International Prize for Writing]] for her book ''The Riddle of the Labyrinth: The Quest to Crack an Ancient Code''.
