"{{DISPLAYTITLE:Lists of ''The New York Times'' Non-Fiction Best Sellers}}\nThis is a list of '''''[[The New York Times]]'' Non-Fiction Best Sellers'''. \n\n[[The New York Times Best Seller list|''The New York Times'' Best Seller list]] was first published without fanfare on October 12, 1931.<ref>''New York Times'', October 12, 1931. 19</ref><ref name=miller>{{cite book |editor=Ezra Greenspan (editor)|title=[[Book History (journal)|Book History]] |volume=Volume Three |chapter=The Best-Seller List as Marketing Tool and Historical Fiction |author=Laura J. Miller |chapterurl=http://books.google.com/books?id=PEZkkbohbtoC&pg=PA290 |publisher=[[Penn State Press]] |year=2000 |pages=286-304}}</ref> It consisted of five fiction and four non-fiction for the New York City region only.<ref name=miller/> The following month the list was expanded to eight cities, with a separate list for each city.<ref name=miller/> By the early 1940s, fourteen cities were included. A national list was created April 9, 1942 in ''[[The New York Times Book Review]]'' (Sundays) as a supplement to the regular paper's city lists (Monday edition).<ref name=miller/> The national list ranked by weighting how many times the book appeared in each city list.<ref name=miller/> A few years later, the city lists were eliminated leaving only the national rankings, which was compiled according to \"reports from leading booksellers in 22 cities,\" a system which remains essentially unchanged to this day (though the specifics have changed).<ref name=miller/>\n\nA separate category for \"Advice, How-To and Miscellaneous\" books was created January 1, 1984. Its number one bestseller (''The Body Principal'' by Victoria Principal) had been number ten and number twelve on the non-fiction lists for the two preceding weeks.<ref>\n\"The New York Times Book
