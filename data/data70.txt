"{{Use mdy dates|date=September 2012}}\n{{Infobox writer\n| name = Vincent Canby\n| image =\n| caption =\n| birth_date = {{Birth date|1924|07|27|mf=yes}}\n| birth_place = [[Chicago, Illinois]], U.S.\n| death_date = {{Death date and age|2000|10|15|1924|07|27|mf=yes}}\n| death_place = [[Manhattan]], New York City, U.S.\n| occupation = Critic, writer\n}}\n'''Vincent Canby''' (July 27, 1924 \u2013 October 15, 2000) was an American [[film criticism|film]] and [[theatre criticism|theatre critic]] who served as the chief film critic for ''[[The New York Times]]'' from 1969 until the early 1990s, then its chief theatre critic from 1994 to 2000. He reviewed more than 1000 films during his tenure there.<ref>{{cite news|last=Canby |first=Vincent |url=http://movies.nytimes.com/ref/movies/reviews/author/rev_auth_canby/index.html |title=Vincent Canby Reviews \u2013 Best Movie Reviews \u2013 Movies \u2013 New York Times |publisher=Movies.nytimes.com |accessdate=2010-05-14}}</ref>\n\n==Life and career==\nCanby was born in [[Chicago, Illinois]], the son of Katharine Anne (n\u00e9e Vincent) and Lloyd Canby.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/29/Vincent-Canby.html |title=Vincent Canby Biography (1924\u20132000) |publisher=Filmreference.com |accessdate=2010-05-14}}</ref> He attended [[boarding school]] in [[Christchurch, Virginia]], with [[novelist]] [[William Styron]]; and the two became friends. He introduced Styron to the works of [[E.B. White]] and [[Ernest Hemingway]]; and the pair [[hitchhike]]d to [[Richmond, Virginia|Richmond]] to buy ''[[For Whom the Bell Tolls]]''.<ref name=\"Carvajal\">{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2000/11/30/arts/recalling-the-civilized-voice-of-a-critic-vincent-canby.html?src=pm|title=Recalling the Civilized Voice Of a Critic, Vincent Canby|last=Carvajal|first=Doreen|date=November 11, 2000|work=The New York Times|accessdate=February 21, 2011}}</ref> After [[World War II|war service]] in the [[Pacific War|Pacific theater]], he attended  [[Dartmouth College]] and then was employed as a [[film critic]] by ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' for six years.<ref name=\"nytimes\"/>\n\nCanby was an enthusiastic supporter of many [[filmmaker]]s, notably [[Woody Allen]], who credited Canby's rave review of ''[[Take The Money and Run]]'' as a crucial point in his career.<ref>[http://movies.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9402EFDF1439EF3BBC4152DFBE668382679EDE \"Take the Money and Run (1969)\"], ''[[New York Times]]'' review by Vincent Canby, August 19, 1969.</ref> He was also heavily critical of some otherwise acclaimed films, such as ''[[Rocky]]'', ''[[The Empire Strikes Back]]'', ''[[Return of the Jedi]]'', ''[[Night of the Living Dead]]'', ''[[After Hours (film)|After Hours]]'', ''[[Blazing Saddles]]'', ''[[A Christmas Story]]'', ''[[Witness (1985 film)|Witness]]'', ''[[Mask (film)|Mask]]'', ''[[The Natural (film)|The Natural]]'', ''[[Rain Man]]'',  ''[[The Exorcist (film)|The Exorcist]]'', ''[[One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest (film)|One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest]]'',<ref>{{cite news|last=Anderson |first=John |url=http://movies2.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9B02E4DE123EE334BC4A51DFB767838D669EDE |title=Movie Reviews, Showtimes and Trailers \u2013 Movies \u2013 New York Times \u2013 The New York Times |publisher=Movies2.nytimes.com |accessdate=2010-05-14}}</ref> ''[[Deliverance]]'', ''[[The Godfather Part II]]'' and ''[[Alien (film)|Alien]]''.\n\nIn the early 1990s, Canby switched his attention from film to theatre; he was named the chief theatre critic in 1994.<ref name=\"nytimes\">{{cite news| url=http://www.nytimes.com/2000/10/16/nyregion/vincent-canby-prolific-film-and-theater-critic-for-the-times-is-dead-at-76.html?pagewanted=5&src=pm | work=The New York Times | title=Vincent Canby, Prolific Film and Theater Critic for The Times, Is Dead at 76 | date=October 16, 2000}}</ref>\n\nThe career of Vincent Canby is discussed in the film ''[[For the Love of Movies: The Story of American Film Criticism]]'' by contemporary critics such as ''[[The Nation]]\u2019''s [[Stuart Klawans]], who talks of Canby\u2019s influence for a quarter century as America\u2019s most prominent \"make-or-break\" critic, and [[A.O. Scott]], who praises his ''New York Times'' predecessor for \"always finding the right tone\" in his reviews.\n\nCanby never married but was, for many years, the companion of [[Penelope Gilliatt]].<ref name=\"guardian\"/> He died from cancer in [[Manhattan]], [[New York City]], on October 15, 2000.<ref name=\"guardian\">{{cite news|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/news/2000/oct/17/guardianobituaries.filmnews|title=Obituary: Vincent Canby|last=Malcolm|first=Derek|date=October 17, 2000|work=The Guardian|accessdate=October 15, 2010|location=London}}</ref> Almost three years later, upon the death of [[Bob Hope]], the late Canby's [[byline]] appeared on the front page of ''The New York Times''. Canby had written the bulk of Hope's [[obituary]] for the newspaper several years before.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.nytimes.com/2003/07/28/obituaries/28WIRE-HOPE.html | work=The New York Times | title=Bob Hope, Comedic Master and Entertainer of Troops, Dies at 100 | date=July 28, 2003}}</ref>\n\n== References ==\n{{reflist}}\n\n== External links ==\n*[http://movies.nytimes.com/movies/critics/vincent-canby/reviews Vincent Canby Reviews] at ''[[The New York Times]]''\n*{{IMDb name|1298522}}\n\n{{Authority control}}\n\n{{Persondata <!-- Metadata: see [[Wikipedia:Persondata]]. -->\n| NAME              =Canby, Vincent\n| ALTERNATIVE NAMES =\n| SHORT DESCRIPTION = American film critic\n| DATE OF BIRTH     =July 27, 1924\n| PLACE OF BIRTH    =[[Chicago, Illinois]]\n| DATE OF DEATH     =October 15, 2000\n| PLACE OF DEATH    =[[Manhattan]], New York City\n}}\n{{DEFAULTSORT:Canby, Vincent}}\n[[Category:1924 births]]\n[[Category:2000 deaths]]\n[[Category:American film critics]]\n[[Category:Cancer deaths in New York]]\n[[Category:Critics employed by The New York Times]]\n[[Category:American film historians]]\n[[Category:American male writers]]\n[[Category:American military personnel of World War II]]\n[[Category:Film theorists]]\n[[Category:Writers from Chicago, Illinois]]\n[[Category:20th-century American historians]]\n[[Category:20th-century American writers]]"
