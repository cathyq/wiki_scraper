{{URL|www.nytco.com}}\n}}\n\n'''The New York Times Company''' is an American media company which publishes its namesake, ''[[The New York Times]]''. [[Arthur Ochs Sulzberger, Jr.]], has served as Chairman since 1997.<ref name=Sulzberger>{{cite news\n|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2008/05/10/nyregion/10marriage.html | title = Times Publisher and His Wife Separate | author = The New York Times| work = [[The New York Times]]| date = May 10, 2008| accessdate = February 2, 2009\n}}</ref><ref>[http://www.nytco.com/company/board_of_directors/Arthur_O_Sulzberger.html \"Arthur Sulzberger, Jr. Biography\"]</ref> It is headquartered in [[Manhattan]], [[New York]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nytco.com/contact_us/index.html\n| title = Contact Us| publisher = The New York Times Company| accessdate = August 28, 2009}}</ref>\n\n==History==\nThe company was founded by [[Henry Jarvis Raymond]] and [[George Jones (publisher)|George Jones]] in New York City. The first edition of the newspaper ''[[The New York Times]]'', published on September 18, 1851, stated: \"We publish today the first issue of the New-York Daily Times, and we intend to issue it every morning (Sundays excepted) for an indefinite number of years to come.\"<ref name=timeline>{{cite web |url=http://www.nytco.com/company/milestones/timeline_1851.html|title=Timeline|accessdate= September 29, 2008| publisher=The New York Times Company}}</ref>\n\nThe company moved into the cable channel industry purchasing a 40%  interest in the Popcorn Channel, a theatrical movie preview and local movie times, in November 1994.<ref>(November 28, 1994). \"[http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-15908200.html The New York Times Co. has decided to enter the cable network business by taking a 40% stake in the soon-to-be-launched Popcorn Channel. (Brief Article)].\" Broadcasting & Cable. NewBay Media LLC. 1994. Retrieved April 27, 2015 from HighBeam Research.</ref>\n\n[[File:Nytimes hq.jpg|thumb|[[The New York Times Building]]]]\n\nThe company completed its purchase of ''[[The Washington Post]]''{{'s}} 50 percent interest in the ''[[International Herald Tribune]]'' (''IHT'') for [[United States dollar|US$]]65 million on January 1, 2003, becoming the sole owner.<ref>{{cite news |first= David D.|last= Kirkpatrick |title=International Herald Tribune Now Run Solely by The Times |url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9F0DEFDC103FF931A35752C0A9659C8B63& |quote= The International Herald Tribune, descendant of an American paper first published in Paris in 1887, is appearing today for the first time under the sole ownership and management of The New York Times Company. \nThe takeover ends an anomalous 35-year partnership between The Times and its domestic competitor The Washington Post that produced a journalistic hybrid consisting mainly of articles and editorials from both papers compiled by editors in Paris. In October, The Times reached an agreement to buy The Post's 50 percent stake in the venture for about $70 million -- in part, The Post said, by threatening to start a rival paper overseas. |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=January 2, 2003 |accessdate= August 29, 2008 }}</ref>\n\nOn March 18, 2005, the company acquired [[About.com]], an online provider of consumer information for [[United States dollar|US$]]410 million. The Company's core purpose is to enhance society by creating, collecting, and distributing high-quality news, information, and entertainment. | publisher =The New York Times Company}}</ref>\n\n==Company stock profile==\nSince 1967, the company has been listed on the [[New York Stock Exchange]] under the symbol NYT. Of the two categories of stock, [[Share (finance)|Class A]] and Class B, the former is publicly traded and the latter is held privately --- largely (nearly 90%) by the descendants of [[Adolph Ochs]], who purchased ''The New York Times'' newspaper in 1896. In 2015, [[Mexicans|Mexican]] billionaire [[Carlos Slim]] became the top New York Times stockholder<ref>http://www.reuters.com/article/2015/01/14/us-new-york-times-warrants-carlos-slim-idUSKBN0KN2M820150114</ref>\n\n==Board of Directors==\nAt the April 2005 board meeting, Class B shareholders elected nine of the fourteen directors of the company.<ref name=2005FR>{{cite web|title=2005 Proxy Statement |url=http://www.nytco.com/pdf/2005_Proxy_Statement.pdf| publisher =The New York Times Company|accessdate= August 23, 2008}}</ref>\n\n== Community awards ==\n[[File:Linda Allen and Gigi Lincoln talk to Janet Robinson.jpg|thumb|2008 ''I Love My Librarian'' award recipients Linda Allen and Margaret \"Gigi\" Lincoln talk with [[Janet L. Only [[Nonprofit organization|nonprofits]] in New York City, [[Long Island]], or [[Westchester County, New York|Westchester]] were eligible.<ref>{{cite press release\n|url=http://www.businesswire.com/portal/site/google/?ndmViewId=news_view&newsId=20070628006103&newsLang=en\n| title = The New York Times Company Announces Four Winners of Its First Nonprofit Excellence Awards\n| publisher = The New York Times Company\n| date = June 28, 2007\n| accessdate = December 10, 2008\n}}</ref>\n\nJointly with the [[Carnegie Corporation of New York]] and the [[American Library Association]], The New York Times Company sponsors an award to honor librarians \"for service to their communities.\" The ''I Love My Librarian!'' award was given to ten recipients in December 2008, and presented by The New York Times Company president and CEO [[Janet L. Robinson]], Carnegie Corporation president [[Vartan Gregorian]], and Jim Rettig, president of the [[American Library Association]].<ref>{{cite press release\n| publisher = American Library
