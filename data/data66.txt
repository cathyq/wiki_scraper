Maslin''' (born August 12, 1949) is an [[United States|American]] [[journalist]], best known as a [[film critic|film]] and [[literary criticism|literary critic]] for ''[[The New York Times]]''.<ref>[http://movies.nytimes.com/movies/critics/Janet-Maslin The New York Times]</ref><ref>[http://www.nytimes.com/2012/12/21/books/janet-maslins-10-favorite-books-of-2012.html?_r=0 The New York Times]</ref> She served as the ''Times''<nowiki>'</nowiki> film critic from 1977 to 1999.<ref name=Elder>Sean Elder, [http://www.salon.com/media/feature/1999/09/23/maslin/ \"Maslin Bails, Critics Rail\"], ''[[Salon (magazine)|Salon]]'', September 23, 1999, accessed December 21, 2007.</ref>\n\n== Biography ==\nMaslin graduated from the [[University of Rochester]] in 1970, with a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] degree and a major in [[mathematics]].<ref>[http://rockcriticsarchives.com/interviews/janetmaslin/janetmaslin.html Interview with Janet Maslin<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> She began her career as a [[rock music]] critic for ''[[The Boston Phoenix]]'' and ''[[Rolling Stone]]''.\n\nMaslin was the long-time [[film critic]] for ''[[The New York Times]]'', serving from 1977 to 1999. Her film criticism career, including her embrace of American [[Independent film|independent cinema]], is discussed in the 2009 documentary film ''[[For the Love of Movies: The Story of American Film Criticism]]''. In the film ''[[Entertainment Weekly]]'' critic [[Lisa Schwarzbaum]] recalls the excitement of having a woman as the lead reviewer at ''The New York Times''.\n\nMaslin continues to review books for ''The New York Times''.<ref>[http://topics.nytimes.com/topics/reference/timestopics/people/m/janet_maslin/index.html \"Book Reviews by Janet Maslin\"], New York Times website</ref> Among the most well-known of her reviews is the 2011 essay on the widowed [[Joyce Carol Oates]]' memoir, ''A Widow's Story,'' which was widely discussed.<ref>\"Unethical, Immoral.
